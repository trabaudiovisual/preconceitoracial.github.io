Racial Prejudice

Due to the export of African slaves in the period of discovery and colonization of Brazil, the Negro has been taxed as inferior by the Brazilian population for simple prejudice.

Prejudice that prevents the black citizen from playing roles in society, from receiving quality education, from being able to get a job vacancy, which for some ends up resulting in a single end: CRIMINALITY.

The age of the whip is over, but we are whipped every day with unattainable words, looks, gestures, and patterns of beauty, by unsuccessful attempts like "You are a white soul black", "Until You're a handsome black "or" You're lucky you did not pull your father's hair ", we're labeled as dirty and lazy as in the classic phrase" black service "and incredible as it may sound We are compared to animals, yes that same, which we ALL as humans share a common ancestor, I think you already discovered right !? He's the monkey himself.

Unfortunately the prejudice is still very recurrent in the lives of many Brazilians, with this work we want to make you aware and do not reproduce in any way racism, as human beings we can not allow anyone else to suffer simply by the color of their skin

After all, what is racism?

According to the dictionary, racism is the theory that upholds the superiority of one group over others, based on a concept of race, particularly advocating the separation of racial groups within a country (racial segregation) or even the extermination of a minority.

https://www.priberam.pt/dlpo/racismo [consultado em 12-06-2017].

Where does it happen?

Por mais que tentemos negar, o racismo é uma cultura muito presente em nosso cotidiano. Mesmo que a nação brasileira seja totalmente miscigenada ainda existe o preconceito nos ambientes escolares e de trabalho, com as mulheres negras e até mesmo entre os próprios negros.

Is Racism Victimism?

Many people interpret the idea of ​​racism as an exaggeration of those who suffer from it, or as if it were a utopian concept that in practice does not happen. However, racism is real and can not be ignored, people have died and still die from it, individuals are despised for being black and even beaten in certain cases. We should never compare someone's pain to ours and try to classify them, just because we do not feel and do not identify does not mean that what one feels is victimism
