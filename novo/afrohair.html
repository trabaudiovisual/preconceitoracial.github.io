The issue is that there is no bad hair or better / more beautiful than the other, there are different types of hair each with its own beauty and they require different care. Ever since we were little, we are influenced to perform chemistry in our hair to turn the volume down or even completely straighten our curls to fit a pattern based on what others think and not because we really wanted to do it by our own will to change. This can be bad for both the health of our hair and our emotional health, leading us not to accept ourselves and low self-esteem for failing to meet the smoothed pattern. Many of us grow up carrying this challenge of not accepting and staying relaxed while others decide to go through the hair transition: a process in which we are slowly cutting off the parts of hair with chemistry or even cutting at once (known as big Chop) hair so that it grows back with its natural texture.

For those who have decided to go through the hair transition: it is not easy. It is a hard process of self acceptance, change, patience, and maturation until the curls grow again and burst our essence and our self-esteem.
